## Project Purpose

Builders of Hope CDC works to create and preserve affordable housing while preventing displacement in historically underserved communities. This project was developed to provide a clear, data-driven understanding of neighborhood change across selected Dallas areas and to support proactive housing interventions.

Specifically, this analysis aims to:
- Identify long-term demographic and housing trends
- Assess housing affordability and market pressure
- Highlight displacement risk and stabilization opportunities
- Support mission-driven housing development and preservation efforts

---

## Study Areas

### West Dallas
West Dallas represents a neighborhood experiencing advanced housing market pressure, characterized by rising property values, declining renter presence, and a shift toward higher-value, owner-occupied housing.

### South Dallas
South Dallas represents a historically disinvested community approaching a critical market transition point, where long-standing economic vulnerability intersects with accelerating housing market activity.

---

## Data & Methodology Overview

The analysis integrates multiple decades of publicly available data to assess neighborhood change over time, including:

- Population and demographic characteristics
- Income distribution and poverty indicators
- Housing tenure and household composition
- Land use patterns and housing stock
- Housing market activity (sales, prices, construction)

Trends are analyzed longitudinally to distinguish structural change from short-term market fluctuations.
